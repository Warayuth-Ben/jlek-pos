\# Cross-Channel Rules



\## Purpose



Defines business rules shared across all service channels.



\---



\## Business Rules



\### 1. Every order belongs to exactly one service channel.



A single order cannot belong to multiple channels simultaneously.



\---



\### 2. Service channels determine operational workflow.



Examples include:



\* Table assignment

\* Delivery information

\* Customer pickup

\* Kitchen processing



\---



\### 3. Service channel selection occurs when the order is created.



Changing the service channel after business processing has started may require business approval.



\---



\### 4. Reports may group or filter data by service channel.



Business reporting treats the service channel as a key analytical dimension.



\---



\### 5. Service channel rules extend, but never replace, the core business rules.



All channel-specific behavior must remain consistent with the common business foundation.
